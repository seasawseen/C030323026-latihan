Exo

EXO is a South Korean boy group formed by SM Entertainment and is recognized as one of the most successful K-pop groups in the world. The group was originally divided into two sub-units, EXO-K focusing on promotion in Korea and EXO-M focusing on the Chinese music market, with each unit singing songs in Korean and Mandarin. Since their debut, EXO has been known for their futuristic concept and use of natural elements such as superpowers in their stories, which makes their identity unique in the K-pop world. The group is also known for their strong vocals, sharp choreography, and ability to incorporate various music genres such as pop, R&B, hip hop, and EDM in their songs. With a loyal fan base called EXO-L, they have won major awards and set records for physical album sales, as well as having a hugely successful world tour. Despite member changes from time to time, EXO is still recognized as one of the leading groups influential in spreading the wave of Hallyu globally. Their popularity is not only limited to music, but also in acting, modeling, and business, with members frequently appearing in television dramas, movies, and commercials.

Background

In 2011, SM Entertainment founder Lee Soo-man revealed plans to debut a new boy band that would be divided into two sub-groups, promoting the same music simultaneously in South Korea and China by performing songs in both Korean and Mandarin. After several lineup changes in December 2011, the group finalized its name as Exo, taken from the word "exoplanet". The news of an upcoming debut from SM Entertainment attracted substantial media attention from within South Korea and internationally, with many comparing Exo to fellow SM boy band TVXQ. It was also reported that Exo would compete with a new girl group from rival YG Entertainment.
